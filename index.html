Hayk Saribekyan

I am an MEng student at the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science department of MIT. I work in the Computational Connectomics Group with Prof. Nir Shavit. My reseach interests are concurrent algorithms and data structures, and their applications in computational neuroscience.

I have received my undergraduate degree in Computer Science from MIT in 2016. As an undergraduate student I have interned at Google and D.E. Shaw & Co. and did undergraduate research in my current (computational connectomics) group.

Feel free to download my CV (as of August 2016).

Research Projects

Currently, I work on concurrent data structures that can be used to store and efficiently query large amounts of connectivity data harvested from mouse cortex.

I have worked in Computational Connectomics Group on developing a high-throughput connectomics pipeline. My project was called Big Data Agglomeration for Connectomics (see poster), for which I received the Anna Poghosyats UROP Award. My research was supported by Landsman Undergraduate Research and Innovation Fund.

    Other Activities

    One of my main interests is teaching. I have participated in two Global Teaching Labs programs: in Italy and in Armenia. Members of MIT Armenian Society, including myself, have played an active role in initiating the program in 2015. So far we have successfully, completed two GTL Armenia Programs.

    My brother and I work work on making short educational videos for school students that teach how to use computation in a variety of problems, such as modelling planet movement. See the website (in Armenian).

    Prior to MIT, I have trained the Armenian national team for International Olympiad in Informatics and was the deputy leader of the team in IOI 2011 in Thailand.

    I am also a passionate hiker (or hayker), and love wandering in Armenian mountains. My other hobbies include playing the flute and playing racket sports.
